2. Where do you operate?
We are currently based in Nairobi, Kenya, with active projects in urban and peri-urban communities such as Eastleigh, Maringo, and Kiambiu. We plan to expand to other regions as we grow.

3. How can I get involved?
You can support us by:
- Volunteering your time and skills
- Donating resources or funds
- Partnering with us as an organization or business
- Spreading awareness about our work
Visit our "Get Involved" page to learn more.

4. What types of projects do you run?
Check our causes page and blog page for the various projects we do

5. How are you funded?
We rely on fundraisers, member contributions, general donations, individual donations, community contributions, grants and partnerships with like-minded organizations. We are committed to transparency and accountability in all our financial practices.

6. Can I donate specific items? (e.g., bicycles, sewing machines, sanitary products)
Yes! We welcome in-kind donations such as:
- Bicycles and repair tools
- Sewing machines and tailoring supplies
- Reusable and biodegradable sanitary products
- Educational materials and digital devices
Contact us to coordinate a donation.

7. Do you offer volunteer opportunities?
Absolutely. We need volunteers for:
- Training and mentorship
- Event support
- Community outreach
- Technical skills (e.g., mechanics, digital literacy)
Fill out our volunteer form to express interest.

8. How do you ensure sustainability in your projects?
We prioritize:
- Community involvement and ownership
- Locally relevant solutions
- Capacity building and training
- Long-term partnerships and follow-up

9. Can I partner with my school/organization/business?
Yes! We welcome collaborations with schools, NGOs, businesses, and government agencies. Partnership opportunities include event co-hosting, resource mobilization, and program expansion.

10. How can I stay updated?
Follow us on social media for regular updates on events, impact stories, and opportunities to get involved.

11. Is Opal Life Initiative registered?
Yes, we are a registered community-based organization complying with all Kenyan regulations.
